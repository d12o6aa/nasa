The impact of climate change on mental healthة

How does climate change affect our mental and psychological health? Find out now and take advantage of the resources available.

Explore more
Natural disasters

Floods and wildfires lead to psychological disorders such as anxiety, depression and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) due to the massive loss of resources and homes.

High temperatures

Rising temperatures increase sleep problems, increase suicide rates, and exacerbate mental illnesses such as anxiety and depression.

Air pollution

Air pollution negatively affects the nervous system and increases the risk of anxiety and depression, especially in children and adolescents.

Spread of diseases

Climate change leads to the spread of infectious diseases, which increases psychological and physical stress, and exacerbates psychological problems.
