Environmental Chain Reactions

Environmental disasters do not only affect our immediate surroundings. The damage to one part of the ecosystem triggers a chain reaction that can disrupt air, water, soil, and the living organisms that depend on them.

Air Pollution

Leads to health issues like asthma and heart disease, and contributes to acid rain, which affects soil and water sources.

Water Contamination

Polluted water sources lead to unsafe drinking water, negatively impacting agriculture, food supply, and health.

Soil Degradation

Damaged soil leads to reduced crop yields and threatens food security, impacting biodiversity and ecosystems.
